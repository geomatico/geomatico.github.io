geospatial software experts

geomati.co is a group of independent experts dedicated to developing geospatial software. This partnership allows us to develop our full potential and provide the best service to our customers.

· Develop projects of any type within the geospatial domain.
· Have ability to successfully complete development of any size.
· Manage with seriousness and dedication each project.

how we work

In geomati.co we work remotely with clients and among ourselves, using technologies that facilitate this form of development projects: video conferencing, desktop sharing, online management, etc.

We have proven that this methodology works for us and our customers as we have developed projects and teach courses in different parts of the world.

We work as a virtual office with frequent interactions between us and the customer, streamlining communications, meetings and development, which in turn reduces costs.

projects

  • Literary Map

    www.mapaliterari.cat/ca

    The Map Literari Català (MLC) is a web mapping application that lets you check different global geographical locations that are associated with texts of Catalan literature.

    Client: Espais Escrits. Xarxa del Patrimoni Literari Català.
    Technologies used: Flask, LeafLet and Bootstrap + jQuery.
    Participants: Fernando González and Jorge Arévalo.

  • Geoposter

    www.geomati.co/geoposter

    Geoposter is a simple and friendly map designed to share geolocated information of all kinds between users of a website or web application.

    Technologies used: Flask, LeafLet and Bootstrap + jQuery.
    Participants: Micho García, Víctor García and Óscar Fonts.

  • Hidrographic demarcation node Galicia-coast

    www.cmati.xunta.es/ide-dhgc/

    Geographic information map about hydrographic and hydrologic spatial data contained in the current Hydrologic Plan Galicia-Coast.

    Client: Xunta de Galicia.
    Technologies used: Flask, LeafLet and Bootstrap + jQuery.
    Participants: Fernando González and Jorge Arévalo.

about us

  • Fernando González

    GIS developer

    I’m a GIS developer since 2003. I’m an enthusiastic about software quality and agile methodologies. Since 2011, I’m a member of geomati.co.

    During these years I’ve been working essentially in Java and Open Source GIS projects, contributing mostly to the gvSIG family: gvSIG CE and the gvSIG association. Additionally, Víctor and me have contributed to the open source community with the development of a specific language for geoprocessing called GGL.

    Today I survive as software-soldier of fortune. If you have a problem, if no one else can help, and if you can find me, maybe you can hire geomati.co.

  • Óscar Fonts

    GIS developer

    GIS developer since 2001.
    In Aurensis participates in GIS and Movility projects implemented in C++, .NET y J2EE. In the Catalan Cartographic Institute, he develops an on line geocoder, participates in the consolidation of WMS services, and updates its web map viewer using open source products and open standards. After that, participates in the organization of the FOSS4G 2010 conference, and gives birth to the first group of Geoinquietos (Geogeeks) in Barcelona.

    Since his establishment as a freelance developer, has collaborated with the Universitat Oberta de Catalunya, and lectured a Spatial Data Infrastructures course in the UNIGIS Master, Universitat de Girona.

  • Micho García

    GIS developer

    With a passion for GIS since many years ago, has developed hisprofessional career always in two directions: Geographical Information Systems (GIS) and programming. He studied Ocean Sciences at the Vigo University, and has devoted his professional live to GIS development.

    He participates in many communities, he’s an active member of Xeoinquedos (the galician geogeeks) group. He defines himself as a craftsman, trying to offer a different vision on software development, retaking an artisan attitude, and contributing with personal engagement to every project. He actually participates in geomati.co.

  • Víctor González

    GIS developer

    Víctor González is a Computer Engineer by the Valencia Politechnical University, UPV. During his studies, he has participated in projects aroung GIS, as GearScape, GGL, gvSIG or 52N WPS. He was awarded with the 52º North Student Innovation Prize in 2009 with the project “SQL Script Profile for 52°North WPS-T”, and has participated in the GSoC 2011 in gvSIG with the project “Integration of GGL2 into gvSIG”. He is currently a freelance and has collaborated with geomati.co in projects as ikimap or GGL2.

  • Jorge Arévalo

    GIS developer

    Jorge Arévalo is a Computer Engineer by Universidad Autónoma de Madrid, UAM. He started developing with PHP, but in 2010, he began collaborating with PostGIS and GDAL, projects, after participating in GSoC 2009, creating the PostGIS Raster GDAL driver. He currently works as freelance and has collaborated with geomati.co, in gvSIG CE project.
