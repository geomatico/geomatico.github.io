A group of experts in open source geospatial software

We are five independent software developers. We develop geospatial tools. We created geomati.co to collaborate among us. Through this collaboration, we give better solutions to our customers, without sacrificing a personalized, direct contact with them.

  • We like to establish a direct and true communication between customers and developers, with no intermediaries.
  • We don't sell a single magical product that solves everything. We master a wide range of open source tools, and we will use those that are the most appropriate in each case.
  • We believe in collaboration, shared knowledge, honesty and transparency. We use and make open source software, and strive to produce high quality code. We are active members in many users and developers communities.

How do we work

In geomati.co we work remotely among ourselves, and usually also with our customers. Thanks to remote work style, which is becoming increasingly common, we have developed projects and taught courses successfully all around the world, without moving from home.

We operate as a distributed team, keeping a streamlined remote communication. Our interactions are frequent, both among ourselves and with our customers. We seek to minimize long, heavy meetings and superfluous travels, and put the focus on the joint design and development of the solutions.

We are freelance developers with years of experience. We like to work together, but we keep our individual identity and independence. We endorse every job with our own professional standing.

What do we do

  • Web & Social Mapping

    GeoPoster
    Catalan Literary Map

    Geoposter is the easiest way to share an annotated map with a group of people, and edit its contents collaboratively.

    The Catalan Literary Map allows you to explore and discover the linkages between territory and the literary expression of dozens of authors, through texts, multimedia elements and tour proposals.

    Customers: Consorci del Besòs, Espais Escrits.
    Technologies: Django, Leaflet, Bootstrap.
    Participants: Micho García, Oscar Fonts.

  • Mobile platform applications

    ikiMap for Android
    Literary Routes on your Mobile

    ikiMap is a free online service for creating and publishing maps. The mobile version for Android was developed by geomati.co, allowing the same functionality as the web, such as map feature editing, from your Android device. It is based on the cartographic visualization engine from gvSIG Mini.

    We have two other projects on the way: a multiplatform application for literary routes, and an application for field data collection with Android tablets

    .

    Customers: ikiMap, Espais Escrits.
    Technologies: Android SDK, gvSIG Mini, Phonegap, Leaflet.
    Participants: Micho García, Víctor González, Fernando González.

  • Professional Geoportals and Intranets

    ICOS Carbon Data Portal

    We have implemented land management systems and geographic data portals in various institutions, from local governments to international organizations.

    We rely on the Open Geospatial Consortium international standards, and the Spatial Data Infrastructure principles.

    Customers: CSIC.
    Technologies: PostGIS, GeoServer, GeoNetwork, OpenLayers, GeoExt.
    Participants: Oscar Fonts, Micho García, Fernando González.




  • Training and workshop materials

    We have taught PostGIS, GeoServer, Web Mapping and SDI courses and professional seminars for various institutions, such as the UNIGIS Professional Master from Universitat de Girona, the National University of Costa Rica, or GeoSUR.

    With the training materials from our courses we launched Geotalleres, a place to share materials which facilitate the elaboration of high quality geospatial courses in Spanish language. Geotalleres is an open project, which also involves people who don't belong to geomati.co.

    Training topics: Linux, SQL databases and PostGIS, Geospatial analysis, GeoServer, Spatial Data Infrastructures and OGC standards.
    Participants: Fernando González, Micho García, Oscar Fonts, Jorge Arévalo, Víctor González.

  • Customization of existing projects

    We can extend your desktop GIS of choice, either QGIS or gvSIG / gvSIG CE, and add the functionality you need to optimize your workflow and processes. AniMove, XYTools and survey2gis are some of the plugins where we have cotnributed recently.

    And, when needed, we will add the features you need to the core opensource tools. Some of our contributions are: coordinate transformations in GeoTools, GDAL PostGIS Raster driver, or Oracle Spatial support in 52north's SOS server.

    Customers: DEIMOS Space, Faunalia, CSGIS, ICC, Geosolutions, Port de Barcelona.
    Participants: Jorge Arévalo, Víctor González, Oscar Fonts, Fernando González.

  • We are GeoGeeks

    You will find us, as participants or promoters, in user mailing lists, specialized blogs, (un)conferences, local gatherings, seminars and workshops... wherever someone talks about free geomatics. We like to share knowledge, also outside geomati.co.

    Even in our spare time, there are who tacke titanic and venturous tasks, such as developing a specific geoprocessing language (GGL2), or integrate gvSIG with GeoTools ( ).

    Please grab our code, use it, and help us improve it. And, if you want, we will be happy to hear from your next project.

The people

  • Fernando González

    GIS developer since 2003

    An enthusiast about software quality and agile methodologies. Since 2011, I’m a member of geomati.co.

    During these years he's been working essentially in Java and Open Source GIS projects, contributing mostly to the gvSIG family: gvSIG CE and the gvSIG association. Additionally, Víctor and him have contributed to the open source community with the development of a specific language for geoprocessing called GGL.

  • Óscar Fonts

    GIS developer since 2001

    He loves diving into maps since he was a child. He started as a GIS developer in Aurensis. During his stay in the Cartographic Institute of Catalonia, he had the opportunity to deploy and consolidate the online geoservices and official viewers for the institution, and was introduced to the world of open source software for geospatial and open standards. Since 2010 he continues his career as a freelance developer. He has also conducted teaching activities at the Open University of Catalunya (UOC) and the Girona University.

    He took part in the organization of the FOSS4G 2010 conference in Barcelona, from which the first Geoinquietos group was created.

  • Micho García

    GIS developer since 2006

    He discovered GIS while studying Marine Science at the University of Vigo. He started then to improve his knowledge in the field, sharing his passion for GIS with another of his interests: the software development.

    In 2006, he started his professional career developing on GE Smallworld, switching soon to the Open Source tools stack, while immersing himself in other languages ​​like JavaScript or Python.

    He has developed tools for different SDIs, viewers for the former MARM and for the IET (Xunta de Galicia).

    Deeply interested in GIS development on mobile platforms, he has collaborated in software development projects on native Android platform, and on multiplatform environments using technologies such as Cordova, HTML5 and CSS3.

    He has been an active trainer during his professional career, giving workshops about free technologies for different organizations such as the University of Costa Rica, the Land Management Program in Honduras, or the UNIGIS Master, where he is in charge of the module named "Sharing Geographical Information in the Network".

  • Víctor González

    GIS developer since 2008

    He develops geospatial sofware since 2008. As a student, he has received awards from 52º North and Google to integrate GGL in gvSIG and the 52º North WPS server. Later, as a graduate in Computing Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Valencia (UPV), he collaborated with geomati.co in different projects, such as ikimap and GGL. Since 2012, he is an active member of the team.

    Currently, he is one of the gvSIG CE core developers and he has contributed to several open source projects like GeoServer, GDAL or QGIS.

  • Martí Pericay

    GIS developer since 2004

    His first employer is a huge consulting company which he does not want to remember, but he starts enjoying programming in Geodata Sistemas. He works there for 8 years, from web developer (specialising in front-end technologies and map servers) to project manager.

    In 2012 he begins his geofreelancing adventure with clients like Privalia, BGEO, the Natural Science Museum of Barcelona, the municipalities of Cerdanyola and Castellbisbal and the Diputació de Barcelona. He works with them as a geo specialist consultant, as a tailored webapps developer and as a geo trainer/teacher.
